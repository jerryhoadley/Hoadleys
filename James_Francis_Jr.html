The Hoadley Family

James Francis Hoadley Jr

JAMES FRANCIS HOADLEY, JR. (James Francis #8, Elias #2, Thomas #1), born 5 October, 1859 in Lewiston, Calif.; died 9 May, 1938 in Eugene, Ore. Married 16 October, 1881 in Cloverdale, Calif. ALTA BELLE NASH, daughter of Robert and Sarah Nash, born 13 October 1861 in Wisconsin; died 5 December, 1924 in Avon, Calif.

In 1884 the City Plot Plan of the City of Cloverdale, Calif., depicted two warehouses owned by the Hoadley family. The Commission Warehouse operated under the jurisdiction of J. F. Hoadley, Jr. He was thirty-two years of age at his father's death in 1891. The Republican-Press newspaper of Ukiah, Calif., in its Friday morning October 13, 1893 issue, had the following ad: "Hot Springs Mill Hoadley and Mc Peak, Proprietors. The Hot Springs Mill, 13 miles from Ukiah, is now running. All orders for lumber filled at short notice. Leave orders with either of the under- signed at their yard offices at the Ukiah Planing Mill. Lumber delivered Free within the Town Limits. Hoadley and Mc Peak."

Shortly thereafter, Mr. Hoadley disposed of his interest in the Planning and Sawmill, and moved with his family to Keswick, Calif., to work for the Mt. Copper Co., as a building and maintenance foreman. When the Mt. Copper Co., moved its operations from Keswick to Martinez, Calif., in 1906, he again moved his family, in order to workat the new site. He continued with the Mt. Copper Co., until his retirement in 1928 at age 69.

About this time, Mr. Hoadley's son-in-law Frank Harris bought 160 acres SW of Cloverdale, Mr. Hoadley built a cabin on the land, and spent his summer months there, into the Deer season, for this is Deer hunting territory. Peculiar to this area are the California Chamois Deer, which are very small, usually "dressing out" about seventy-five pounds. This particular type of Deer travels in flocks and browse on grass, not unlike sheep.

During the remainder of the year, Mr. Hoadley lived in Martinez. In 1936 he moved to Eugene, Oregon to live with his son, William Leslie Hoadley, until his death in 1938 at 79 years of age.
