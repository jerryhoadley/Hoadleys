ELIAS HOADLEY (Thomas #1), born 11 August 1770 in Tewksbury, Massachusetts; died 29 March 1843 in Hartland, Vermont; married, (1st) about 1793, SARAH FRENCH, born 29 February 1773; died 17 May 1816 in Hartland. He married, (2nd), in 1817, MRS. MEHITABLE (MARCY) WHITNEY, daughter of Willard and Lydia (Pike) Marcy, born 1 May 1790 in Grantham, N.H.; died 11 February 1864 in Hartland. 

Elias Hoadley settled in Hartland on a wild tract of land of three hundred acres. 

He cultivated this until 1838, when he sold it and removed to a smaller farm in the same town, where he resided until his death five years later. He was an industrious farmer and a kind and honest man.

Elias Hoadley and his first wife Sarah, his second wife Mehitable, and his son Jesse, are all buried in the same Cemetery, in Jenneville, where his Father Thomas and Mother Mary are buried. The family plot is located at the rear of the Cemetery. As stated before the gravestones are in remarkably good condition. The inscriptions are easily read and are as follows: (From left to right). 

ELIAS HOADLEY-DIED-MARCH 29, 1843-AG 73; MEHITABLE-WIFE OF ELIAS HOADLEY- DIED-FEB. 11,1864-AG 74 YRS; IN MEMORY OF MRS. SALLY-WIFE OF MR. ELIAS HOADLEY-WHO DEPARTED THIS LIFE-MAY 17, 1816- AGED 43 YEARS.; IN MEMORY OF JESSE-SON OF ELIAS & SALLY HOADLEY-WHO DIED-DEC. 28, 1813-AGED 17 YRS. 

To the left of Elias Hoadley's headstone, are two small markers, one with the initials "S.H." and the other with "J.H.". It might be assumed that these could be the gravesites of Elias and Sally's first child Sally Hoadley Hodgman and her husband Jonathan Hodgman. 

The cellar hole of Elias's home can be found on a little traveled road, southeast of So. Woodstock. Property records are to be found at the County Clerk's office at Damon Hall in Hartland, Windsor County, Vt.
