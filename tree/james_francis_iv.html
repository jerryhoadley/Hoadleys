The Hoadley Family

James Francis Hoadley IV

JAMES FRANCIS HOADLEY IV (James Francis III #51, James Francis, Jr. #43, James Francis #8, Elias #2, Thomas #1), born 21 January, 1914 in San Ramon, Calif. Married 16 January, 1938 in Riverside, Calif., to ELOISE EDNA BOTHWELL, daughter of Allison and Hazel Anna (Booth) Bothwell, born 8 March, 1914 in Breckenridge, Missouri.

James Francis received his schooling in Perris and Riverside, Calif.

Mr. and Mrs. Hoadley were married in a candlelight ceremony in All Saints Episcopal Church in Riverside with Reverend Henry Clark Smith officiating. Mrs. Hoadley was attended by Miss Blanche Chaney, a classmate of hers in Nurses training at St. Luke's Hospital in Boise, Idaho. Phil Hoadley was best man and William Hoadley, an usher, The reception was held at Mrs. Hoadley's Aunt and Uncle's, Dr. and Mrs. W. H. Thompson, home in Riverside, CA. The Hoadleys at this time were to resided in Riverside, CA.

Mr. Hoadley was employed by Southern Pacific Railroad in Los Angeles and later until World War II he was employed by the Associated Produce Dealers and Brokers of Los Angeles, Inc. He enlisted in the U.S. Army during World War II, and was discharged after spending three and one half years in Africa and the ETO.

After his military service he returned to his employment with the Associated Produce Dealers and Brokers of Los Angeles, Inc., and today holds the position of Executive Vice-President of this organization.

Mrs. Hoadley, at the time of her marriage, was employed as Office Nurse for Dr. W. W. Catherwood, Jr. In later years she worked at the Long Beach Hospital, from which institution she recently retired after serving as Superintendent of Nurses.

Their children born in Los Angles, CA
